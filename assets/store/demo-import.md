## Spaghetti Carbonara

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients

- 200 g spaghetti
- 100 g pancetta or smoked bacon
- 2 eggs + 2 egg yolks
- 50 g pecorino romano, finely grated
- 50 g parmesan, finely grated
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

### Instructions

1. Cook spaghetti al dente in heavily salted water.
2. Fry pancetta in a dry pan until golden, about 5 min. Remove from heat.
3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, yolks, and grated cheese. Season generously with pepper.
4. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
5. Toss hot pasta into the pan with pancetta (heat off). Add 3 tbsp of cooking water.
6. Add the egg mixture and stir vigorously — the sauce should be creamy, not scrambled.
7. Add more cooking water if needed. Serve immediately.

### Notes

The key is residual heat only — never put the pan back on the stove or the eggs will scramble.

## Wild Mushroom Risotto

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 3

### Ingredients

- 300 g arborio rice
- 30 g dried wild mushrooms
- 1 onion, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 150 ml dry white wine
- 1 l vegetable stock, kept warm
- 40 g butter
- 60 g parmesan, grated
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Salt, pepper, fresh parsley

### Instructions

1. Soak dried mushrooms in 300 ml warm water for 20 min. Drain, reserving the liquid. Chop mushrooms.
2. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft, about 5 min. Add rice and toast for 2 min, stirring.
3. Pour in wine and stir until absorbed.
4. Add warm stock one ladle at a time, waiting for each addition to absorb before adding the next.
5. After 10 min, add mushrooms and the strained soaking liquid.
6. Cook for 18–20 min total until rice is creamy and al dente.
7. Remove from heat, stir in butter and parmesan. Rest for 2 min.
8. Season, sprinkle with parsley, and serve immediately.

## Classic Tiramisu

**Prep:** 30 min | **Servings:** 6

### Ingredients

- 500 g mascarpone
- 4 eggs, separated
- 100 g icing sugar
- 200 ml strong espresso, cooled
- 3 tbsp amaretto or rum
- 200 g savoiardi ladyfinger biscuits
- Cocoa powder for dusting

### Instructions

1. Beat egg yolks with icing sugar until pale and fluffy.
2. Add mascarpone and mix until smooth.
3. Beat egg whites with a pinch of salt to stiff peaks. Gently fold into the mascarpone mixture.
4. Combine espresso and alcohol in a shallow dish.
5. Briefly dip each ladyfinger in the coffee mixture (1–2 seconds per side — don't over-soak).
6. Arrange a layer of biscuits in a dish, spread half the cream on top.
7. Repeat with remaining biscuits and cream.
8. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
9. Dust generously with sifted cocoa powder before serving.

### Notes

It tastes even better the next day — the flavours meld beautifully overnight.

## Thin-Crust Margherita Pizza

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 12 min | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients

**Dough:**
- 250 g tipo 00 flour (or bread flour)
- 7 g instant yeast
- 160 ml lukewarm water
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tbsp olive oil

**Sauce and toppings:**
- 200 g tomato passata
- 1 clove garlic
- Salt, dried oregano, pinch of sugar
- 150 g fresh mozzarella, drained
- Fresh basil, olive oil to finish

### Instructions

1. Mix yeast with warm water and sugar. Let stand 5 min.
2. Combine flour and salt, add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead for 8 min.
3. Cover and leave in a warm place for 1 hour until doubled.
4. Preheat oven to maximum temperature (250°C+) with the baking tray inside.
5. Mix passata with crushed garlic, salt, oregano and a pinch of sugar.
6. Roll out the dough very thin on baking paper.
7. Spread sauce, add torn mozzarella.
8. Bake 10–12 min until crust is golden and crisp.
9. Top with fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil right after baking.

## Classic Apple Pie

**Prep:** 30 min | **Cook:** 50 min | **Servings:** 8

### Ingredients

**Pastry:**
- 350 g plain flour
- 200 g cold butter, cubed
- 100 g icing sugar
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 tsp baking powder
- Pinch of salt

**Filling:**
- 1.5 kg tart apples (e.g. Granny Smith)
- 4 tbsp sugar
- 2 tsp cinnamon
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 1 tbsp potato starch

### Instructions

1. Rub flour and butter together until it resembles breadcrumbs. Add sugar, yolks, baking powder and salt — quickly bring together into a dough.
2. Divide into two thirds and one third, wrap in cling film and chill for 30 min.
3. Peel and coarsely grate the apples. Mix with sugar, cinnamon, lemon juice and starch. Squeeze out excess juice.
4. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease a 24 cm tin.
5. Press the larger dough portion into the base and sides of the tin.
6. Spread the apple filling evenly.
7. Grate the smaller dough portion over the top.
8. Bake 45–50 min until the top is golden.
9. Cool completely before slicing. Dust with icing sugar.

### Notes

Best served the next day — the flavours settle beautifully. Serve with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.

## Chocolate Pudding

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 4

### Ingredients

- 500 ml whole milk
- 3 tbsp cocoa powder
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 3 tbsp cornstarch
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 50 g dark chocolate, chopped (optional)

### Instructions

1. Whisk together cold milk, cocoa, sugar and cornstarch in a saucepan until completely smooth.
2. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly.
3. Once it starts to thicken, cook for another 2 min on low heat.
4. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla and chopped chocolate if using.
5. Pour into glasses or ramekins. Press cling film directly onto the surface to prevent a skin forming.
6. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

## Panna Cotta with Raspberry Coulis

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 5 min | **Servings:** 4

### Ingredients

**Panna cotta:**
- 500 ml heavy cream (30%)
- 50 g sugar
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 10 g gelatine leaves (or 2 tsp powdered gelatine)

**Raspberry coulis:**
- 200 g raspberries, fresh or frozen
- 2 tbsp sugar
- Juice of half a lemon

### Instructions

1. Soak gelatine leaves in cold water for 5 min (or prepare powdered gelatine as instructed).
2. Heat cream with sugar and vanilla until almost boiling — do not let it boil.
3. Remove from heat, squeeze out gelatine leaves and stir into the hot cream until dissolved.
4. Pour into moulds or glasses. Cool, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
5. Coulis: cook raspberries, sugar and lemon juice over low heat for 5 min. Strain or leave chunky.
6. Unmould onto a plate (or serve in glasses) and spoon raspberry coulis over the top.

## Tomato Soup with Rice

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 4

### Ingredients

- 1 l chicken stock
- 500 g tomato passata
- 1 onion
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1 carrot
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 150 g white rice
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Sugar, salt, pepper
- Fresh basil or parsley

### Instructions

1. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft. Add grated carrot and cook for 3 min.
2. Add tomato paste and cook for 1 min, stirring.
3. Pour in stock and passata. Bring to a boil.
4. Add rice and cook for 15–18 min until tender.
5. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity.
6. Serve with fresh basil or parsley and crusty bread.

## Baked Salmon with Roasted Vegetables

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients

- 2 salmon fillets (about 180 g each)
- 2 courgettes
- 1 red bell pepper
- 1 yellow bell pepper
- 200 g cherry tomatoes
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Thyme, rosemary, salt, pepper

### Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
2. Cut vegetables into chunks, spread on a baking tray. Drizzle with 3 tbsp olive oil, season with salt, pepper and herbs.
3. Roast vegetables for 10 min.
4. Season salmon with salt and pepper, drizzle with olive oil. Place on top of the vegetables.
5. Squeeze lemon juice over everything. Scatter sliced garlic around.
6. Bake for another 15 min — salmon should be just slightly pink in the centre.
7. Serve immediately, optionally with couscous or crusty bread.

## Chicken in Creamy Mushroom Sauce

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 3

### Ingredients

- 600 g chicken breast
- 400 g button mushrooms, sliced
- 200 ml soured cream or crème fraîche
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 tbsp plain flour
- Salt, pepper, thyme, fresh parsley

### Instructions

1. Cut chicken into strips, season with salt and pepper. Sear in butter on both sides until golden, about 5 min. Set aside.
2. In the same pan, sauté onion until soft. Add garlic and mushrooms, cook until all liquid has evaporated, about 8 min.
3. Sprinkle with flour and stir. Pour in cream and 100 ml water.
4. Return chicken to the pan. Simmer on low heat for 10 min, stirring occasionally.
5. Season to taste, sprinkle with parsley. Serve with pasta or mashed potatoes.

## Red Lentil Dal

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 4

### Ingredients

- 250 g red lentils
- 1 can chopped tomatoes (400 g)
- 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
- 1 onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cm fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tbsp oil
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1/2 tsp chilli flakes
- Salt, fresh coriander or parsley, rice to serve

### Instructions

1. Heat oil in a pot. Add cumin and sizzle for 30 seconds. Add onion and cook for 5 min.
2. Add garlic, ginger and remaining spices. Cook for 1 min, stirring.
3. Add canned tomatoes and cook for 3 min.
4. Rinse lentils and add along with coconut milk. Stir to combine.
5. Simmer on low heat for 20–25 min until lentils are soft and the sauce has thickened.
6. Season with salt. Serve over rice with fresh coriander.

### Notes

Tastes even better the next day. Freezes beautifully — make a double batch.

## Banana Oatmeal with Nuts

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 5 min | **Servings:** 1

### Ingredients

- 50 g rolled oats
- 200 ml milk (or plant-based alternative)
- 1 ripe banana
- 1 tbsp peanut butter
- Handful of walnuts or almonds
- 1 tsp honey
- Pinch of cinnamon

### Instructions

1. Combine oats and milk in a small saucepan. Heat over medium, stirring, for 4–5 min.
2. Once thickened, remove from heat.
3. Slice the banana — mash half into the oatmeal, reserve the rest.
4. Transfer to a bowl. Top with remaining banana slices, peanut butter and nuts.
5. Drizzle with honey and dust with cinnamon.

## Fried Eggs on Sourdough with Avocado

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 5 min | **Servings:** 1

### Ingredients

- 2 eggs
- 2 slices sourdough or rye bread
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1/2 ripe avocado
- Salt and pepper
- Chives or sprouts to garnish
- Chilli flakes (optional)

### Instructions

1. Toast the bread.
2. Melt butter in a pan over medium heat.
3. Crack in the eggs and fry for 3–4 min (whites set, yolks runny) or to your liking.
4. Mash avocado with a fork, season with salt and pepper.
5. Spread avocado on toast, top with the fried eggs.
6. Scatter chives and chilli flakes on top. Season with salt and pepper.

## Ricotta Pancakes

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients

**Batter:**
- 200 g plain flour
- 2 eggs
- 250 g ricotta
- 150 ml milk
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp baking powder
- Pinch of salt
- Butter for frying

**To serve:**
- Fresh berries
- Maple syrup or honey
- Icing sugar

### Instructions

1. Whisk together eggs, ricotta, milk and sugar until smooth.
2. Fold in flour, baking powder and salt — mix until just combined (small lumps are fine).
3. Heat a knob of butter in a non-stick pan over medium-low heat.
4. Pour small ladles of batter into the pan. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, about 2–3 min.
5. Flip and cook for another 1–2 min until golden.
6. Serve with fresh berries, a drizzle of maple syrup and a dusting of icing sugar.

### Notes

Don't overmix the batter — lumps are fine and keep the pancakes fluffy.
